Banana Cake

Introduction to making Banana Cake Banana cake is the ultimate comfort dessert, celebrated for its incredibly moist, dense crumb and deep, caramelized sweetness. Unlike its more modest cousin, banana bread, a true banana cake is refined with a lighter texture and a more delicate balance of spices like cinnamon or nutmeg. The magic of the recipe lies in the use of overripe, blackened bananas, which provide not only a potent aromatic punch but also natural sugars and moisture that keep the cake tender for days. It is a rustic yet soul-satisfying bake that transforms simple pantry staples into something deeply flavorful and aromatic. Often topped with a thick layer of tangy cream cheese frosting or a decadent brown butter glaze, banana cake bridges the gap between a casual afternoon snack and a sophisticated celebration dessert. The addition of buttermilk or sour cream in many recipes ensures a tangy counterpoint to the intense sweetness of the fruit, while the inclusion of toasted walnuts or pecans can add a much-needed crunch to the soft, "melt-in-your-mouth" interior. It is a testament to the idea that some of the best flavors in baking come from ingredients that look like they’ve seen better days.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 Cups of Milk
  • Cooking spray
  • 3/4 (1 1/2 sticks) butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup packed brown sugar
  • 3 eggs
  • 3 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 3/4 cup sour cream
  • 1 tsp. pure vanilla extract
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 Tbsp. cornstarch
  • 1 tsp. baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp. ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp. kosher salt
  • 1 (8-oz.) block cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) butter, softened
  • 1 tsp. pure vanilla extract
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup walnuts, roughly chopped

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350° and line two 8" cake pans with parchment paper and grease with cooking spray. In a large bowl, cream together butter and sugars until smooth. Add eggs, one at a time, beating well after each one, then add vanilla. Add bananas and sour cream and beat until smooth.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, cornstarch, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt. Add dry ingredients to wet ingredients and beat until just combined.
  3. Divide evenly between prepared pans and bake until tops are slightly golden and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean, 30 to 35 minutes.
  4. Let cool 15 minutes, then invert onto cooling rack to cool completely.
  5. In a large bowl with a hand mixer, beat together cream cheese and butter until smooth. Add powdered sugar and beat again until smooth then add vanilla and heavy cream and beat until a spreadable consistency.
  6. Spread about half the frosting onto one layer, top with second layer, then frost top. Garnish with banana and walnuts.
