Red Velvet Cake

Introduction to making Red Velvet Cake
Red velvet cake is a culinary icon known as much for its dramatic, crimson hue as its sophisticated flavor profile. Unlike a standard chocolate cake, red velvet relies on a specific chemical interplay between acidic buttermilk, vinegar, and natural cocoa powder. This combination tenderizes the gluten in the flour, resulting in a crumb that is exceptionally fine, smooth, and—as the name suggests—velvety. The cocoa flavor is intended to be a subtle, mellow undertone rather than an aggressive chocolate punch, allowing the tangy notes of the buttermilk and cream cheese frosting to take center stage. Historically, this cake gained its reddish tint from the reaction of old-fashioned cocoa with acidic ingredients, though modern recipes typically use food coloring to achieve that signature electric glow. It occupies the perfect middle ground between a light sponge and a dense butter cake, making it the go-to choice for celebrations where you want something that feels both indulgent and elegant. Whether served as a towering layer cake or a simple tray of brownies, it remains a timeless classic that balances sweetness with a distinct, savory tang.

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ cups sugar
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp unsweetened cocoa powder.
  • 1 cup buttermilk (room temp)
  • 2 large eggs,
  • 1 ½ cups vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp white distilled vinegar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 tbsp red food coloring

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch cake pans
  2. Whisk flour, sugar, baking soda, salt, and cocoa powder in a large bowl.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat the eggs, oil, buttermilk, food coloring, vanilla, and vinegar until smooth.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet. Mix until just combined—do not overbeat!
  5. Pour evenly into pans and bake for 30–35 minutes
  6. Once completely cool, frost with a mixture of 16oz cream cheese, ½ cup butter, 4 cups powdered sugar, and 1 tsp vanilla.
